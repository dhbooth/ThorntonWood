Mailboxes are the responsibility of the unit owner. 

For a lost key, locksmiths will not make extra keys unless you have an original key with the number on it.  Without a key, a new lock must be ordered. It's a good idea to make a record of your mailbox key code (5 digit number on the key.)

For a new lock, you can order from the website: www.mailboxes.com/ OR call 800-MAILBOX (624-5269). Our mailboxs are the 2200 series. The part number for the lock is 2290, and includes two keys; to order extra keys, note that the part number is 2298.

Please note that certain lock troubles may be avoided by annually squirting a lubricant into the lock mechanism from the outside.  Then, insert the key and operate the lock to distribute the lubricant.

The Association maintains a Master Insurance Policy on the Common Elements, Limited Common Elements, as well as the units for typical perils. In addition, the Board has secured insurance for water back-up coverage, with a limit of $ 50,000 on each of the six buildings and will cover damage to the buildings if (1) water or sewage should back-up through the sanitary waste lines due to a clog, or (2) water came up through the sump pit because of a power failure or inoperative pump. Please note that this added insurance does NOT cover ground water or water which might enter through the foundation. The cost of the aforementioned insurance coverage is a common expense which is included in the quarterly assessments.

The individual unit coverage under the Master Policy includes the structure and all components as originally constructed up to and including the walls, ceilings, roof, original floors, and other original painting, appliances, fixtures, and etc..  

The initial deductible on the Master Policy is $5,000 (as of 4/2013).  In other words, the Association is self-insured for the first $5,000 of any covered loss. The deductible becomes a common expense should a covered loss be incurred.  However, as of June 1, 2009, the Maryland condominium law was amended to provide that if a loss covered under the Association’s master policy originates within a unit, the owner will be responsible for the insurance deductible up to $5,000; any loss above that limit is covered by the Association.

Unit owners are encouraged, not required, to separately obtain coverage for personal property within the units as well as improvements and betterments (including any and all improvements made to the original construction, e.g. upgraded cabinets, appliances, wall coverings, carpets, added rooms or baths, etc.).  In Maryland, this policy is referred to as a HO-6 standard form. Unit owners are advised to add a rider for replacement cost and another to cover liability for the deductible should a loss originate in your unit.  Please consult your insurance agent to help you decide how much of each coverage to have in your unit owner's policy.
